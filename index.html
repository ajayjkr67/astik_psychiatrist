Services

We provide comprehensive, evidence-based psychiatric care for children, adults, and older adults. Our approach focuses on accurate diagnosis, compassionate treatment, and long-term wellbeing.

Child & Adolescent Disorders

Early diagnosis & developmental care.

Learning Disorder

Learning disability refers to difficulty in skills like reading, writing, or mathematics despite normal intelligence. With early identification and support, children can learn effectively and build confidence.

Autism Spectrum Disorder

Autism is a developmental condition affecting communication, social interaction, and behaviour. Early intervention and supportive therapies help individuals reach their full potential.

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

ADHD causes problems with attention, impulsivity, and restlessness that affect academic and social functioning. Proper treatment helps children improve focus, behaviour, and self-esteem.

Adult Mental Health Disorders

Mood, anxiety, and psychotic disorder management.

Depression / Anxiety

Anxiety and depression involve excessive worry, fear, or nervousness and persistent low mood, loss of interest, and reduced energy that interfere with daily functioning. Both are medical conditions and respond well to appropriate treatment.

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD involves unwanted repetitive thoughts and compulsive behaviours performed to reduce anxiety. With appropriate treatment, people can gain good control over symptoms.

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia affects thinking, perception, and behaviour, causing symptoms like hallucinations and delusions. Early diagnosis and sustained treatment significantly improve long-term outcomes.

Geriatric Mental Health Disorders

Cognitive, emotional, and age-related mental health care.

Dementia

Dementia leads to a gradual decline in memory, thinking, and daily functioning beyond normal ageing. Early diagnosis helps in planning care and improving quality of life.

Depression in Older Adults

Depression in the elderly may present with sadness, withdrawal, or physical complaints. It is treatable and should not be considered a normal part of ageing.

Delirium

Delirium is a sudden state of confusion caused by medical illness, infections, or medications. It is a medical emergency and usually reversible when treated promptly.

Our Approach

A thorough clinical evaluation that includes detailed history, symptom assessment, and appropriate screening tools to arrive at an accurate diagnosis and clear treatment plan.
Careful selection of medications based on established clinical guidelines, with regular follow-up to monitor response, side effects, and long-term effectiveness.
Use of structured, evidence-based therapies to help patients understand their condition, manage symptoms, and develop healthier coping strategies.
Involving family members to improve understanding of the illness, enhance treatment adherence, and create a supportive home environment.
Treatment tailored to each patient’s needs, considering emotional, social, and functional aspects along with medical care.
